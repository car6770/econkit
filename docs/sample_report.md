# Sample Economic Report

This is an example of the type of report that EconKit can generate from a macroeconomic dataset.

## Dataset overview

- Number of observations: 10
- Available years: 2015 to 2024

## Summary statistics

The dataset includes basic macroeconomic indicators such as GDP growth, inflation, unemployment, and interest rates.

Students can use these indicators to practice basic economic data analysis with Python.

## Indicator highlights

### GDP Growth

GDP growth shows how the economy expands or contracts over time.

In the sample dataset, students can compare periods of positive growth with periods of economic slowdown.

### Inflation Rate

Inflation measures changes in the general price level.

Students can use the inflation series to understand how price pressures change over time.

### Unemployment Rate

The unemployment rate helps describe labor market conditions.

Students can compare unemployment with GDP growth and inflation to practice basic macroeconomic interpretation.

### Interest Rate

Interest rates are useful for studying monetary policy conditions.

Students can compare interest rates with inflation and GDP growth to understand basic macroeconomic relationships.

## Educational use

This sample report is intended for economics students who are learning how to move from raw economic data to a simple written analysis.

EconKit helps students practice:

- Loading economic data
- Creating summary statistics
- Visualizing economic indicators
- Comparing macroeconomic variables
- Writing a basic economic interpretation
